## 2026-06-09 - CWE-200 Profiling Endpoint Exposure via `http.DefaultServeMux`
**Vulnerability:** The AWS, GCP, and Posix entry points were inadvertently exposing the `http.DefaultServeMux`'s `/debug/pprof` endpoints due to anonymous imports of `net/http/pprof` and `/debug/vars` from `expvar`. This exposed sensitive application metrics and profiling information, enabling potential Denial of Service (DoS) and reconnaissance attacks by unauthorized users.
**Learning:** `net/http/pprof` and `expvar` automatically register themselves with `http.DefaultServeMux` via their `init()` functions when imported. If a public-facing service uses this multiplexer without proper authentication or restricted access, these profiling endpoints become accessible to the world.
**Prevention:** Avoid anonymous imports (`_`) of `net/http/pprof` and `expvar` in production entry points. If profiling or metrics are required, explicitly register them on a dedicated, internal-only `http.ServeMux` or use dedicated tooling like OpenTelemetry (which is already present in this project).
