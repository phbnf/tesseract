## 2024-05-10 - CWE-200 Profiling Endpoint Exposure in DefaultServeMux
**Vulnerability:** The public-facing entry points `cmd/tesseract/gcp/main.go` and `cmd/tesseract/posix/main.go` mistakenly imported `net/http/pprof` and `expvar`. This exposed the `/debug/pprof/*` and `/debug/vars` endpoints on the global `http.DefaultServeMux` to the public internet, potentially leading to a CWE-200 (Information Exposure) vulnerability.
**Learning:** Importing these packages purely for their side effects (registering handlers on `DefaultServeMux`) is dangerous in production services exposed to untrusted networks. They should only be enabled on dedicated, internal administrative endpoints or ports.
**Prevention:** Avoid blanket `_ "net/http/pprof"` and `_ "expvar"` imports in public-facing binary entry points. If profiling or metrics are required, explicitly configure them on a separate `http.ServeMux` bound to an internal network interface. Use OpenTelemetry for application metrics instead of relying on `expvar` for production observability.
