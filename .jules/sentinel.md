## 2025-01-20 - [Exposure of Profiling and Metrics Endpoints (CWE-200)]
**Vulnerability:** The codebase had unused imports of `net/http/pprof` and `expvar` in `cmd/tesseract/gcp/main.go` and `cmd/tesseract/posix/main.go`. These packages automatically register endpoints like `/debug/pprof/*` and `/debug/vars` on the `http.DefaultServeMux`. This leads to the unintended exposure of profiling and internal metrics, which might leak sensitive execution or configuration details to the outside world.
**Learning:** Automatically registered HTTP endpoints on `http.DefaultServeMux` pose a major risk if the server binds directly to it or unintentionally exposes it. Even simple blank imports (e.g. `_ "net/http/pprof"`) can instantly expose a wealth of internal diagnostic information, turning into a CWE-200 (Exposure of Sensitive Information to an Unauthorized Actor).
**Prevention:** Avoid blank imports for diagnostic packages like `net/http/pprof` and `expvar` in main application binaries that handle external traffic unless strictly necessary and protected by authentication or network segmentation. Instead, explicitly register handlers on a separate, dedicated `http.ServeMux` mapped to an internal port or loopback interface, ensuring public-facing multiplexers are isolated.
