## 2024-05-20 - [CWE-200] Profiling Endpoint Exposure
**Vulnerability:** Anonymous imports of `net/http/pprof` (in `cmd/tesseract/gcp/main.go` and `cmd/tesseract/posix/main.go`) and `expvar` (in `cmd/tesseract/posix/main.go`) automatically register profiling and variable endpoints on `http.DefaultServeMux`. These endpoints `/debug/pprof/*` and `/debug/vars` get exposed alongside the main server routes, leading to CWE-200 information exposure vulnerabilities.
**Learning:** In Go, anonymous imports (using `_`) of `net/http/pprof` or `expvar` intentionally cause side effects in their `init()` functions, modifying global state like `http.DefaultServeMux`. When `http.ListenAndServe` or `http.Server` uses a custom mux or `http.DefaultServeMux` that handles root (`/`), these endpoints become publicly accessible if not explicitly restricted.
**Prevention:** Avoid anonymous imports of `net/http/pprof` and `expvar` in production entry points. If profiling or metrics are required, explicitly bind their handlers to a separate, internal-only HTTP server, or utilize secure, non-exposing telemetry mechanisms like OpenTelemetry.
